About Me

I am a resilient and highly organized designer/developer who creates captivating, user friendly interfaces that enable users to experience the essence of a company digitally.


I am a sophomore at the University of Southern California working towards a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Game Development with a minor in Web Development. I am eagerly pursing a position as a tech-based product designer or software developer, as I have an eye for creativity and am passionate about the technology of the future. Through my current UX position and independent work, I have a knack for evolving the user expereince through unique designs and website development. You can find me researching new programming techniques for hours or deeply analyzing user data, all to construct the most efficient design for the task at hand, and even pitch such ideas to senior leaders.


In my free time I often find myself doodling tattoo concepts or watching YouTube to learn new applications of programming. I also work hard to stay active, usually by hitting the gym or taking a hike at the nearest natural park. I enjoy exploring new places and traveling with my boyfriend, wrapping up a nice day with a few episodes of South Park.


I am a dedicated and determined student who is continuously searching for new opportunities that will allow me to advance as a Product Designer or Software Developer, all while enjoying every step of the way.

Core Values

Inclusion

With my past, current, and future work I aspire to create designs that are not only accessible to all, but also promote the inclusion of minority groups while browsing the web.

Curiosity

As a designer I am always searching for new programs to further my creative abilities. I hope to educate others on my progress and my work done in website design or development.

Outreach

When designing, I have the opportunity to create pages seen across the web. These designs should have a great influence on users, working to promote a positive message.

About NMDesign

The projects depicted are products of work done for various internships I have had and from independent work done on my own time.
